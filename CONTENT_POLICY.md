**Last updated: April 11, 2026**

## 1. Intended Use

rAIdio.bot is designed for creating original music, audio, and voice content. The Software is a creative tool. Like any tool, its value depends on how it is used. This policy describes what responsible use looks like and what is prohibited.

You are solely responsible for ensuring your use of generated content complies with applicable laws, platform terms of service, and the rights of third parties. The developer is not liable for content you create, distribute, or publish.

## 3. Voice Cloning and Conversion

The Software includes voice cloning and voice conversion features. These are powerful capabilities that carry serious ethical and legal obligations.

You must:

- Obtain explicit, informed, and documented consent from any person whose voice you clone or convert before doing so
- Disclose to audiences when synthetic voice content is used, where required by law or platform policy
- Comply with all applicable laws governing synthetic voice content, including emerging synthetic media disclosure laws in your jurisdiction
- Not use voice features to impersonate any person for deceptive, fraudulent, or harmful purposes
- Not create synthetic voice content designed to mislead listeners into believing they are hearing a real, unaltered recording
- Not create non-consensual synthetic voice content of any kind, including content of a sexual, defamatory, or harassing nature

Consent obtained under duress, deception, or without full understanding of the intended use does not satisfy these requirements.

If the voice you are cloning, converting, or training on is your own, you may proceed without third-party consent. By submitting audio to the Software's voice features and completing the consent attestation, you affirm that every voice present in that audio is either (a) your own voice, or (b) covered by explicit, informed, and documented permission you have obtained from each voice owner. You remain solely responsible for producing that documentation if your authorization is challenged.

## 4. Music, Copyright, and Third-Party Material

You are responsible for clearing all rights necessary for your intended use of content you create. This includes:

- Ensuring any samples, stems, or reference audio you import into the Software are licensed for your intended use
- Complying with applicable mechanical, synchronization, and performance license requirements when distributing your output
- Not using the Software to reproduce, reconstruct, or closely imitate copyrighted musical works without appropriate licensing

The Software generates original audio. It does not contain or reproduce copyrighted recordings. C2PA content credentials document the provenance of AI-generated components. This documentation supports but does not substitute for your own rights clearance obligations.

---

## 5. AI Model Training Data

The AI models included with the Software are trained on licensed and public domain material. The developer does not train models on content you create with the Software. Your output is yours and is not used to train or improve any model without your explicit consent.

---

## 6. Prohibited Uses

You may not use the Software to:

- Infringe the intellectual property rights of any person or entity
- Generate audio deepfakes intended to deceive, defraud, defame, harass, or harm any person
- Impersonate real individuals, living or deceased, in a manner likely to deceive or damage their reputation
- Produce non-consensual intimate audio content of any person
- Create content designed to facilitate illegal activity
- Produce content that constitutes hate speech, incitement to violence, or harassment targeting individuals or groups
- Circumvent platform detection systems in a manner that violates those platforms' terms of service
- Create synthetic evidence intended for use in legal proceedings without disclosure

---

## 7. Synthetic Media Disclosure

An increasing number of jurisdictions require disclosure when synthetic media is used in certain contexts, including political advertising, commercial content, and news media. You are responsible for understanding and complying with disclosure requirements in your jurisdiction and in the jurisdictions where you distribute your content. C2PA content credentials embedded by the Software provide a machine-readable disclosure mechanism and are strongly recommended.

---

## 8. C2PA Content Credentials

We strongly recommend keeping C2PA signing enabled. Content credentials provide a verifiable, tamper-evident record that your content was created with a legitimate tool using ethically documented models. This benefits you as a creator by establishing provenance and benefits audiences by enabling informed consent about the nature of the content they are consuming.

Deliberately removing, altering, or suppressing C2PA credentials on content you distribute may violate the terms of this policy and, in some jurisdictions, applicable law.
